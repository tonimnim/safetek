We've been heads-down shipping software for clients for the better part of three years, and we've quietly built a body of opinions about how to make products work in this region. Most of those opinions never made it past a slack thread.

This journal exists to fix that.

## What we're going to write about

Three buckets, mostly:

- **Engineering at the edge of the network.** Most of our users are on $40 phones and 3G. That changes how you think about state, sync, and graceful degradation. We'll share what works.
- **Product decisions on tight budgets.** Our clients aren't series-B startups. They're operators running real businesses on real margins. We'll talk about how that constrains — and clarifies — product choices.
- **Boring infrastructure that earns its keep.** We've cut hosting bills by half for clients more than once. Sometimes the right answer is a tiny VM. We'll write about when, and why.

## What we won't write about

We won't be writing about whatever framework Twitter is currently arguing about. We won't write about LLMs unless we've shipped something with one and it actually moved a number. We won't pretend our work is harder or more sophisticated than it is.

## Cadence

Roughly biweekly. If we don't have something useful to say, we'll skip it.

Subscribe via the [contact form](/#contact) and we'll let you know when new posts go up.
